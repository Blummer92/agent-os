# Material Design Tokens And Components

Companion to `../material-design-defaults.md`.

## Token Baseline

- heading and body fonts: clean, readable sans serif
- heading size: clearly larger than body text
- body size: easy to read in print and on screen
- image scale: medium by default
- margins and line spacing: moderate
- section spacing: generous enough to separate tasks clearly
- borders: simple and low-clutter
- colors: restrained, classroom-safe, and comfortable to read
- writing space: larger than minimal default
- worksheet density: moderate to low

## Layout Defaults

- Prefer checkboxes, labeled sections, compact tables, cue boxes, and chunks over dense prose.
- Use repeatable section patterns when they improve student scanning.
- Keep directions, examples, practice, and reflection predictable.
- Favor visible writing space over squeezing in extra prompts.
- Keep repeated components in consistent locations when materials are connected.

## Scaffolding Defaults

Use moderate embedded scaffolding by default: chunking, signaling, concise
teacher cues, short headings, sentence frames when needed, checkboxes, progress
markers, and close pairing of related text and visuals.

Prefer embedded supports over separate differentiated versions unless requested.
Fade scaffolds when students have enough practice.

## Reusable Components

Reuse proven classroom components when appropriate:

- learning targets
- success criteria
- checklist boxes
- worked example cards
- vocabulary callouts
- reflection boxes
- critique stems
- task cards
- comparison frames

Use a component only when it improves clarity, pacing, or evidence collection.

## Discussion Support

Include discussion support when it helps students process design choices,
explain thinking, compare options, or engage in partner critique.

Use sentence stems when they improve participation or explanation quality. Keep
discussion support lighter when the task is mostly individual, procedural, or
quick-turn practice.
