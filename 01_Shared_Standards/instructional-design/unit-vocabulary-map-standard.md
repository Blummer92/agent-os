# Unit Vocabulary Map Standard

## Purpose

Use this standard before lesson-level vocabulary planning so agents know which words are already known, newly taught, introduced only, reused across units, or saved for later.

This supports `student-language-standard.md`, `unit-alignment-rules.md`, `teacher-modeling-standards.md`, `instructional-materials-workflows.md`, `slide-deck-defaults.md`, and `material-quality-rubric.md`; it does not replace them.

## Vocabulary Categories

- Review Vocabulary: already taught in a prior unit and reused here.
- Teach Vocabulary: core words taught and practiced in this unit.
- Introduce, Don’t Assess Yet: words students may hear or see but are not accountable for yet.
- Transfer Vocabulary: words reused across units to support durable Digital Media habits.
- Future Vocabulary: words saved for a later unit or deeper lesson sequence.

## Source Order

1. Check unit alignment before lesson packets.
2. Check lesson packets before inventing new vocabulary.
3. Treat inferred vocabulary as proposed, not approved.
4. If prior context is unavailable, write `Prior context not provided`.

## Difficulty And Reuse Rules

- Lower difficulty when a word was already taught in a prior unit.
- Mark words as Transfer Vocabulary when they recur across units.
- Do not reteach Review Vocabulary as brand-new unless evidence shows students need it.
- Do not assess Introduce, Don’t Assess Yet or Future Vocabulary.

## Language Placement Rules

Separate teacher language from student-facing language.

- Teacher Language Use may include precise academic or planning language the teacher explains aloud.
- Student Language Use must be understandable, usable, and appropriate for students.
- Slide/Worksheet Safe words must be short enough, clear enough, and supported enough for student-facing materials.
- Words that appear only in teacher talk should not be assessed until students have practiced using them.

## Handoff Rule

When passing to lesson planning, include the vocabulary category, prior-unit connection, assessment status, and slide/worksheet safety for any words that affect student language.
