# Teacher Modeling Misunderstanding Audit Template

## Purpose

Use this template to audit a lesson, model, or teaching sequence for likely
student misunderstandings in the style of an alignment playbook.

## Context Fields

- lesson, day, or section:
- objective:
- student task:
- expected student product:
- success criteria:
- grade level or course:
- materials to review:

## Audit Task

1. Identify the highest-probability student misunderstandings.
2. Ground each misunderstanding in the task, directions, model, vocabulary, and expected thinking.
3. Explain why students may misunderstand.
4. Describe what the teacher would likely notice.
5. Evaluate whether current modeling, directions, or supports already address it.
6. Flag gaps, vague language, or modeling that does not prepare students enough.
7. Suggest stronger teacher moves, modeling, and exact replacement language when useful.

## Output Structure

For each likely misunderstanding, include:

- potential student misunderstanding
- why students may think this
- what the teacher would likely notice
- current teacher support already present
- gap or problem in the current model or support
- stronger teacher modeling move
- exact teacher language to use
- optional check for understanding

## Additional Directions

- Stay close to actual lesson materials instead of giving generic advice.
- Focus on the highest-leverage misunderstandings, not every possible mistake.
- Prioritize misunderstandings that would block successful task completion.
- Distinguish what students misunderstand, why they misunderstand it, and what the teacher should say or do next.
- If a section is already strong, say so briefly.
- If the fix is obvious, rewrite the exact teacher language directly.
- Organize section by section, day by day, or slide by slide when the source supports that structure.

## Optional Ending

End with:

- top 3 misunderstandings to prioritize
- top 3 teacher-modeling revisions to make first

## Suggested Use

Use for lesson plans, teacher models, slide decks, curriculum guides, day-by-day
sequences, and teacher-facing notes. It works best when the source materials show
the objective, student task, and current model or directions.
